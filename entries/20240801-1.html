A legitimiate, innovative use for AI art?

20240801-1

The current state of AI art makes it so that reality is unable to be fully represented 1:1. It can get close, but it almost feels like a pastiche of the work it's emulating/deriving from.

AI is constantly used to disinform. Could that be its "flagship"? The act of disinforming is inherent to its medium.

  1. IT CANNOT ACCURATELY REPRESENT INFORMATION on a technical level
  2. IT IS USED TO REPRESENT THE REALITY OF SOMETHING, BUT DUE TO 1., it inherently cannot do so, being misinforrmation by a technicality.

There is an uncanny feeling aobut AI art. Vicerally, humans look at it and can (usually) tell.

Maybe the act of the work and experiencing are twofold:

  1. The creation is simulating. It is used to attempt emulating reality. It pretends, and attempts to be real to the viewer. OR/AND...
  2. The creation is playing a role. Because of inherent flaws in representing reality, all involved in the experience understand its lack of reality despite it best efforts. All understand its "fakeness", its emulation.

Maybe the fakeness is its inherent form. It's uncanny, it tries to edge closer to reality but crucially must fail. By a technicality, failing to be reality is misinforrmation. A viewer knows it to be false. The creator too.

"The medium is the message." ITS ATTEMPT TO REPRESENT REALITY IS THE SIGNIFICANCE, THE MEANING, THE EXPERIENCE.
